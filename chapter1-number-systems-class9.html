Real Numbers

Real numbers include both rational and irrational numbers.

Irrational Numbers

Numbers that cannot be written in the form p/q (where p, q are integers and q≠0) are irrational. Example: √2, π.

Decimal expansion of irrational numbers is non-terminating and non-recurring.

Laws of Exponents

a^m × a^n = a^(m+n)

(a^m)^n = a^(mn)

a^0 = 1

Solved Examples

Example 1: Rationalize the denominator

Question: Rationalize 1/√2

Solution: Multiply numerator and denominator by √2. (1×√2)/(√2×√2) = √2/2.

Example 2: Exponents

Question: Simplify 2^2 × 2^3

Solution: 2^(2+3) = 2^5 = 32.
