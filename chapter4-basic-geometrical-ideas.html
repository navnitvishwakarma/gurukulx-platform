Introduction to Geometry

Geometry has a long and rich history. The term 'Geometry' is the English equivalent of the Greek word 'Geometron'. 'Geo' means Earth and 'Metron' means Measurement. In this chapter, we will learn about the basic building blocks of geometry.

Points, Lines, and Rays

  • Point: A point determines a location. It has no size, no width, no length, and no depth. It is usually denoted by a capital letter like A, B, C.
  • Line Segment: The shortest distance between two points is called a line segment. It has two endpoints. Denoted as AB.
  • Line: A line is obtained when a line segment is extended indefinitely in both directions. It has no endpoints. Denoted as AB (with arrows).
  • Ray: A ray is a portion of a line. It starts at one point (called starting point) and goes endlessly in a direction. Example: Sun rays. Denoted as OA (arrow on one side).

Angles

An angle is made up of two rays starting from a common starting point. The two rays forming the angle are called the arms or sides of the angle. The common starting point is the vertex of the angle.

Polygons

A polygon is a simple closed curve made up entirely of line segments.

  • Triangle: A polygon with 3 sides.
  • Quadrilateral: A polygon with 4 sides.

Circles

A circle is a simple closed curve which is not a polygon. Key parts:

  • Center: Fixed point in the middle.
  • Radius: A line segment connecting the center to a point on the circle.
  • Diameter: A line segment passing through the center and connecting two points on the circle. Double the radius.
  • Chord: A line segment connecting any two points on the circle.

Solved Examples

Example 1: Identifying Shapes

Question: Name the points and line segments in the figure (imagine a triangle ABC).

Solution:

Points: A, B, C

Line Segments: AB, BC, CA

Example 2: Understanding Circles

Question: If the radius of a circle is 5 cm, what is its diameter?

Solution:

We know that Diameter = 2 × Radius

Given Radius = 5 cm

Diameter = 2 × 5 = 10 cm

Answer: 10 cm
