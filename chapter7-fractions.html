Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole or generally, any number of equal parts.

Parts of a Fraction

In a fraction a/b,

  • a is the Numerator (the top part).
  • b is the Denominator (the bottom part).

Example: In 3/4, 3 is the numerator and 4 is the denominator.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fraction: Numerator is less than denominator (e.g., 2/5). Value < 1.
  • Improper Fraction: Numerator is greater than or equal to denominator (e.g., 5/3). Value ≥ 1.
  • Mixed Fraction: Combination of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 1 2/3).
  • Equivalent Fractions: Fractions that same value (e.g., 1/2, 2/4, 3/6).

Operations

Addition/Subtraction (Same Denominator): Add/subtract numerators, keep denominator same.

Simplest Form: A fraction is in simplest form if the numerator and denominator have no common factor except 1.

Solved Examples

Example 1: Identifying Fractions

Question: Express 'three-fifths' as a fraction.

Solution:

3/5

Example 2: Adding Like Fractions

Question: Add 2/7 + 3/7

Solution:

(2 + 3) / 7 = 5/7
