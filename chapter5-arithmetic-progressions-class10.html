Arithmetic Progressions

nth term, sum of n terms, and sequence properties

0% Complete

Sequences and Series

An arithmetic progression (AP) is a list of numbers in which each term is obtained by adding a fixed number to the preceding term except the first term.

General Form

a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ...

a is first term, d is common difference.

Formulas

nth term (an) = a + (n-1)d

Sum of n terms (Sn) = n/2 [2a + (n-1)d] or n/2 [a + an]

Solved Examples

Example 1: Find 10th term

Question: 2, 7, 12, ... Find 10th term.

Solution: a=2, d=5. a10 = 2 + (10-1)5 = 2 + 45 = 47.

Example 2: Find Sum

Question: Sum of first 10 natural numbers.

Solution: Sn = 10/2 [1 + 10] = 5 × 11 = 55.
