Measuring Shapes

In this chapter, we will learn how to measure lines and angles, and classify different shapes based on their properties.

Angles

We classify angles based on their measure:

  • Acute Angle: Less than 90°.
  • Right Angle: Exactly 90°.
  • Obtuse Angle: Greater than 90° but less than 180°.
  • Straight Angle: Exactly 180°.
  • Reflex Angle: Greater than 180° but less than 360°.

Triangles

Triangles are classified based on sides and angles.

Based on Sides:

  • Scalene: All sides are different.
  • Isosceles: Two sides are equal.
  • Equilateral: All three sides are equal.

Based on Angles:

  • Acute Angled: All angles are acute.
  • Right Angled: One angle is a right angle.
  • Obtuse Angled: One angle is obtuse.

Quadrilaterals

  • Rectangle: Opposites sides equal, all angles 90°.
  • Square: All sides equal, all angles 90°.
  • Parallelogram: Opposite sides parallel and equal.
  • Rhombus: Parallelogram with all sides equal.
  • Trapezium: One pair of parallel sides.

3D Shapes

Shapes that have length, breadth, and height are called 3D shapes.

  • Cube, Cuboid, Cylinder, Sphere, Cone, Prism, Pyramid.

Solved Examples

Example 1: Classifying Triangles

Question: A triangle has sides 5cm, 5cm, and 8cm. What type of triangle is it?

Solution:

Since two sides are equal (5cm and 5cm), it is an Isosceles Triangle.

Example 2: Identifying Angles

Question: What type of angle is 105°?

Solution:

105° is greater than 90° and less than 180°.

Therefore, it is an Obtuse Angle.
