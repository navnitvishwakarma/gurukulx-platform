Introduction to Large Numbers

In this chapter, we will learn about large numbers and how to read, write, and compare them. We'll also understand the concept of place value.

What are Large Numbers?

Large numbers are numbers that are greater than 1000. They help us count and measure things in our daily life like:

  • Population of cities
  • Distances between places
  • Amount of money
  • Number of books in a library

Place Value System

The place value system helps us understand the value of each digit in a number based on its position.

1 2 3 4 5
Ten Thousands Thousands Hundreds Tens Ones

Number Names

Every number has a name. Here are some examples:

1,000 = One Thousand
10,000 = Ten Thousand
1,00,000 = One Lakh
10,00,000 = Ten Lakh
1,00,00,000 = One Crore

Solved Examples

Example 1: Reading Large Numbers

Question: Read the number 45,678

Solution:

45,678 = Forty-five thousand six hundred seventy-eight

4 is in ten thousands place = 40,000
5 is in thousands place = 5,000
6 is in hundreds place = 600
7 is in tens place = 70
8 is in ones place = 8
Total = 40,000 + 5,000 + 600 + 70 + 8 = 45,678

Example 2: Comparing Numbers

Question: Compare 25,430 and 25,340

Solution:

Step 1: Compare ten thousands place → Both have 2

Step 2: Compare thousands place → Both have 5

Step 3: Compare hundreds place → 4 > 3

Therefore: 25,430 > 25,340
