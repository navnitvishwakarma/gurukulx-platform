Solving Systems of Equations

We deal with two linear equations in two variables, x and y.

Methods

  • Substitution: Solve one equation for one variable and substitute into the other.
  • Elimination: Add or subtract equations to eliminate a variable.

Consistency Conditions

a₁/a₂ ≠ b₁/b₂ => Unique solution (Intersecting lines).

a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂ => Infinite solutions (Coincident lines).

a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂ => No solution (Parallel lines).

Solved Examples

Example 1: Substitution

Question: x+y=14, x-y=4.

Solution: From second, x=y+4. Sub in first: y+4+y=14 => 2y=10 => y=5. x=9.
