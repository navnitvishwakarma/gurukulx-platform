Continuity and Differentiability

Limits, continuity, and derivatives of functions

0% Complete

Continuity

A function f is continuous at x=c if lim(x->c) f(x) = f(c).

Differentiability

f is differentiable at c if limit (f(x)-f(c))/(x-c) exists.

Chain Rule: d/dx (fog) = f'(g(x)) * g'(x).

Standard Derivatives

d/dx (x^n) = nx^(n-1)

d/dx (sin x) = cos x

d/dx (e^x) = e^x

Solved Examples

Example 1: Continuity

Question: Check continuity of f(x) = |x| at x=0.

Solution: LHL = 0, RHL = 0, f(0) = 0. Continuous.

Example 2: Derivative

Question: Find derivative of sin(x^2).

Solution: Chain rule: cos(x^2) * 2x = 2x cos(x^2).
